This guide serves as a central repository for internship search strategies, preparation resources, application trackers, and specific program guidelines.

## 1. Overview & Strategy

Securing a high-impact internship involves a blend of technical preparation, strategic networking, and contribution to high-quality projects.

- **Focus Areas:** Deepening core knowledge (data structures, algorithms, system design) and practical development skills.
- **Timeline:** Application seasons usually begin 6–8 months prior to the internship start date.
- **Application Channels:**
  - Standard corporate internship programs.
  - Direct cold outreach & referrals (refer to [COLD_OUTREACH.md](file:///c:/Users/govin/OneDrive/Desktop/opensrc/govind-os/templates/COLD_OUTREACH.md)).
  - Structured open-source mentorships & fellowships.

## 2. Open Source Mentorships & Fellowships

Structured programs are a highly respected pathway to build real-world software engineering experience.

### Major League Hacking (MLH) Fellowship
The **MLH Fellowship** is a 12-week internship alternative where fellows collaborate on real-world projects under the guidance of professional mentors.

- **Tracks:**
  - **Open Source:** Contribute to major open-source projects (e.g., React, Kubernetes).
  - **Software Engineering:** Build commercial-grade projects for corporate partners.
  - **Site Reliability Engineering (SRE):** Master system administration, monitoring, and cloud-native tools.
- **Application & Interview Process:**
  - **Initial Screen:** Review of GitHub presence, project quality, and essays.
  - **Technical Interview:** A review of a code sample you submitted. You must explain your code, design decisions, and how you would improve it.
  - **Behavioral Interview:** Assess collaborative skills, motivation, and culture fit.
- **Preparation Tips:**
  - Ensure you have a strong, clean code sample written entirely by you.
  - Be ready to describe how your code sample works line-by-line.

## 3. Preparation Roadmap

A systematic approach to interview readiness.

### Core Technical Foundations
- **Algorithms & Data Structures:** Regular practice on platforms like LeetCode. Focus on key patterns: Two Pointers, Sliding Window, Trees, Graphs, and Dynamic Programming.
- **System Design:** Understand basic web service architectures, load balancing, caching, and databases (see [SYSTEM_DESIGN.md](file:///c:/Users/govin/OneDrive/Desktop/opensrc/govind-os/engineering/SYSTEM_DESIGN.md)).
- **Language Proficiency:** Master at least one language deeply (e.g., [GO.md](file:///c:/Users/govin/OneDrive/Desktop/opensrc/govind-os/engineering/GO.md)).

### Resume & Applications
- Review and refine your resume using the guidelines in [RESUME_RULES.md](file:///c:/Users/govin/OneDrive/Desktop/opensrc/govind-os/career/RESUME_RULES.md).
- Keep a clean, well-documented portfolio of personal and open-source projects on GitHub.
